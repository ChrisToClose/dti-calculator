Departing Property (FM0132)
FM0132 is the Fannie Mae guideline for clients keeping their current home as a rental. Lenders can only count 75% of gross rent to offset departing property expenses — the 25% haircut accounts for vacancy and maintenance. If 75% of rent doesn't cover expenses, the shortfall is added to back-end DTI as a monthly debt. Enter $0 rent if no lease is signed yet — full expenses still count.

PMI Estimator JUMBO
PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) is required on conventional loans when your down payment is below 20%. It protects the lender — not you — and is based on your loan amount, LTV ratio, and credit score. It cancels automatically at 78% LTV. Think of it as the cost of buying with less than 20% down — it disappears, unlike rent.
On a $785k home with 15% down: 760+ FICO pays ~$100/mo PMI. A 680 score at the same price and down pays ~$394/mo. Credit score matters significantly.

DTI Qualification Gauge
Front-End
Front-end DTI = Housing Payment ÷ Gross Monthly Income. Counts only home costs: P&I, taxes, insurance, HOA, flood, PMI. Generally around 28–31% for CONV and FHA.
—
Back-End
Back-end DTI = (Housing + All Debts + Rental Shortfall) ÷ Income. The number lenders focus on most. Limits: CONV 50%, FHA 57%, VA 41% guideline, USDA 46%.
